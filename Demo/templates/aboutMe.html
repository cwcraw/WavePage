About Me

Chip Crawford is a software engineer living in Tokyo, Japan. Before leaving California for Japan, he worked in the semiconductor industry. In this industry, he supported the implementation of cutting edge techniques to improve metrology and quality control. Wanting to transition to a career that provided a more direct connection to the technologies that are changing people’s lives, he attended Code Chrysalis Coding Bootcamp . At Code Chrysalis he learned the basics of web development and developed an interest in presenting numerical information clearly on the web. Since graduation he has been working as a software engineer in Tokyo.

Chip attended The College Of William and Mary for undergraduate and received his PhD at UC, Berkeley working under Professor Alex Pines . For more details on his education and work experience please visit his LinkedIn Page.

Outside of work, Chip’s interests include spending time with his family, reading obscure history, working on coding projects, biking and rock climbing. Once his familial responsibilities settle down he looks forward to volunteering again - in the past having worked in soup kitchens and teaching in prisons .

The repository for this page and other examples of his independent coding projects can be found at his GitHub page.
